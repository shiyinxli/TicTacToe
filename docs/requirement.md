# Persona 1: Moritz Schneider

![father photo](boss.jpg)

## Basic Information

* Name: Moritz Schneider  
* Age: 45  
* Occupation: IT Manager at a tech company in Frankfurt  
* Education: Master’s degree  
* Family: Married, one daughter (Mia, primary school student)  
* Preferred Language: German
* Role: Father player / first player
* Device: computer / smartphone
* Browser Skill: proficient
* Typical Network: Mobile internal / Company Ethernet / Company WLan
* Communication while Playing: Voice call / Whatsapp


**Background & Characteristics**  
 Moritz works full-time as an IT manager and has a demanding schedule, often working until late in the evening. Because of his busy job, he has limited time to spend with his daughter during weekdays. He is technically skilled and comfortable using desktop computers and web applications.

**Goals & Motivation**  
 Moritz wants to use his lunch break efficiently to connect with his daughter. He is looking for a simple and quick way to play a game together remotely, without installing any software.

**Needs**

* A fast, easy-to-use browser-based game  
* Compatibility with desktop (office computer)  
* Minimal setup (no installation, no login complexity)  
* Clear game status and smooth interaction

**Frustrations**

* Complicated setup processes  
* Slow or unreliable applications  
* Interfaces that are not intuitive

# Persona 2: Mia Schneider
![daughter photo](daughter.jpg)
## Basic Information
* Name: Mia Schneider
* Age: 6
* Role: Child player / second player
* Device: Smartphone
* Browser Skill: Basic
* Typical Network: Mobile internet or school Wi-Fi
* Communication While Playing: Voice call on WhatsApp
**Background & Characteristics**  
Mia is a primary school student who likes simple mobile games. She can tap buttons, open links, and use basic apps, but she has a low tolerance for complex menus and long loading times.  
**Goals & Motivation**
Mia wants to play TicTacToe with her parents during a school break. She joins from her smartphone, needs to understand the game state immediately, and expects a full game to finish within a few minutes.
* Play TicTacToe quickly during short breaks.
* See whose turn it is at all times.
* Complete one game in a few minutes.  
**Needs**
Browser-based gameplay optimized for small screens.
Large touch targets and clear game symbols.
No registration or account setup.
Fast loading and stable connection behavior.
Immediate feedback after sending a move.  
**Frustrations**
Small buttons that are difficult to tap.
Confusing layout or unclear game state.
Account creation before playing.
No confirmation that a move was sent.
Lost game state after browser refresh.


# User Journey: Moritz Schneider

1. Moritz opens his browser on his office computer during lunch break.

2. He navigates to the TicTacToe web application.

3. The game loads instantly without requiring installation or login.

4. He starts a new game session and shares access with Mia (e.g., via link or code).

5. Moritz waits for Mia to join the game.

6. Once connected, the game begins automatically.

7. Moritz takes turns placing his marks on the grid.

8. He sees real-time updates when Mia makes her move.

9. The game displays the result (win/lose/draw).

10. Moritz resets the game to play another round if time allows.

# User Journey: Mia Schneider
 1. Mia opens the browser on her smartphone during a school break.

  2. She receives the game link or room code from her father on WhatsApp.

  3. She taps the link and enters the TicTacToe web app.

  4. The page loads quickly without installation or account login.

  5. Mia taps “Join Game” or enters the room code to connect.

  6. She sees a clear status message showing a connection/waiting state.

  7. Once both players are connected, the match starts automatically.

  8. When it is her turn, Mia taps a grid cell to place her mark.

  9. She sees real-time updates when Moritz makes his move.

  10. At the end, the game shows win/loss/draw, and she can start another round if time allows.
